Frankenstein

by Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley

A Digital Edition

Welcome to the mini digital edition of Mary W. Shelley's Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus (1818), made as a project for Text as Data II, taught at the University of Antwerp. This digital edition contains the first draft of Chapter 7A, written by Mary W. Shelley in 1816, with corrections by both Mary W. Shelley and her husband and fellow-writer Percy B. Shelley. This edition is based on the Bodleain MS Abinger c.56 manuscript.

About the manuscript

Bodleian MS Abinger c.56 (formerly designated Dep. 477/1) consists of 62 leaves originally bound in Notebook A, one of two now disbound notebooks in which Mary W. Shelley drafted the two-volume version of Frankenstein. The draft in c.56 runs from the beginning to the end of Volume I, concluding just as the Creature is about to begin his tale to Victor.

The manuscript is located at the Bodleian Libraries at the University of Oxford and can be accessed in full online through The Shelley-Godwin Archive.
