Frankenstein

by Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley

A Digital Edition

Welcome to the mini digital edition of Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley's Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus (1818), made by Merel Naudts as a project for Text as Data II, taught at the University of Antwerp. This digital edition contains the first draft of Chapter 7A, written by Mary W. Shelley in 1816, with corrections by both Mary W. Shelley and her husband and fellow-writer Percy B. Shelley. This edition is based on the Bodleain MS Abinger c.56 manuscript.

About the manuscript

Bodleian MS Abinger c.56 (formerly designated Dep. 477/1) consists of 62 leaves originally bound in Notebook A, one of two now disbound notebooks in which Mary W. Shelley drafted the two-volume version of Frankenstein. The draft in c.56 runs from the beginning to the end of Volume I, concluding just as the Creature is about to begin his tale to Victor.

The manuscript is located at the Bodleian Libraries at the University of Oxford. It can be accessed in full online through The Shelley-Godwin Archive, which has digitalised the manuscripts of Mary W. Shelley, her husband Percy B. Shelley and her parents Mary Wollstonecraft and William Godwin. The archive offers a digital view on the impressive work of "England's first family of writers".

About the Shelleys

Mary Wollstonecraft Shelley (1797-1851) was an English Romantic novelist. She is most known for writing the Gothic novel Frankenstein, which is considered one of the first science fiction novels. She was married to Percy Bysshe Shelley (1792-1822), one of England's major Romantic poets, whose oeuvre includes poems such as "Ozymandias" (1818) and "To a Skylark" (1820).

In the dreary summer of 1816, Mary and Percy visited Lord Byron in his villa by Lake Geneva. To pass the time, Lord Byron challenged the company to each write a ghost story. Thus, the idea for Frankenstein was born.
